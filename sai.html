Introduction Ihram Tawaf Sa'i & Tahallul

Pillar 3: Sa'i

Sa'i is the ritual walking seven times between the hills of Safa and Marwah, commemorating Hajar's search for water for her son Ismail.

How to Perform Sa'i

Step 1: Start at Safa

After drinking Zam Zam water, proceed to the hill of Safa. Ascend it, face the Ka'bah, and recite:

إِنَّ الصَّفَا وَالْمَرْوَةَ مِن شَعَائِرِ اللَّهِ
بے شک صفا اور مروہ اللہ کی نشانیوں میں سے ہیں
Innaṣ-Ṣafā wal-Marwata min sha'ā'irillāh.
(Indeed, Safa and Marwah are among the symbols of Allah.) (Surah Al-Baqarah 2:158)

Make personal supplications at Safa.

Step 2: Walk to Marwah

Walk towards Marwah. Between the two green pillars, men should walk briskly (but not run). Upon reaching Marwah, ascend it, face the Ka'bah, and make dua. This completes one round.

Step 3: Return to Safa

Walk back to Safa in the same manner. This is the second round.

Step 4: Complete Seven Rounds

Continue until you complete seven rounds, ending at Marwah.

Note: If you forget the count, assume the lesser number and complete the remainder.

Pillar 4: Tahallul

Tahallul is the act of shaving or cutting the hair, which symbolically releases you from the state of Ihram.

How to Perform Tahallul

For Men

It is highly recommended to shave the entire head. At minimum, you must trim a portion of hair from all over the head.

For Women

Women must only trim their hair, approximately the length of a fingertip. They do not shave their heads.

Completion: Once Tahallul is completed, all restrictions of Ihram are lifted, and your Umrah is complete.

Completion of Umrah

Congratulations! You have completed all the pillars of Umrah. For the Mehdavi believer, this is not an end, but a beautiful new beginning. It is a chance to return to daily life with a heart purified, a faith renewed, and a spirit reconnected to the Divine.

May Allah accept your journey, forgive your sins, and make this Umrah a source of immense spiritual light and guidance for you. May you carry the peace and blessings of the Sacred Mosque in your heart forever.

رَبَّنَا تَقَبَّلْ مِنَّا ۖ إِنَّكَ أَنتَ السَّمِيعُ الْعَلِيمُ
اے ہمارے رب! ہم سے قبول فرما، بے شک تو ہی سننے والا جاننے والا ہے
Rabbana taqabbal minna innaka antas-Sami'ul-'Alim.
(Our Lord, accept [this] from us. Indeed, You are the Hearing, the Knowing.) (Quran 2:127)
